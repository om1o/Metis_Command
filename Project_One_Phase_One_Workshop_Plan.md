# Project One Phase One: Defining Mission, Vision, and Strategic Objectives

**Objective:** To articulate a clear, inspiring mission statement, a compelling vision statement, and 3-5 measurable long-term strategic objectives for the agent company.

**Participants:** Key stakeholders, including leadership, department heads, and potentially a few long-standing, insightful employees from various functions. (Aim for 5-10 participants for effective discussion).

**Duration:** Two half-day workshops, ideally spaced a few days apart to allow for reflection.

## Workshop 1: Mission and Vision Statement Development (Half-Day)

**Materials Needed:** Whiteboard or flip charts, markers, sticky notes, pens, prepared guiding questions.

**Agenda:**

- **1. Introduction & Context (15 mins):**
    - Briefly review the purpose of the strategic assessment and the importance of mission/vision.
    - Explain the workshop's goal: to collaboratively draft initial mission and vision statements.

- **2. Understanding "Mission" (60 mins):**
    - **Definition:** "Why do we exist? What do we do, for whom, and what is the impact?"
    - **Guiding Questions for Discussion:**
        - What is the core business of our agent company?
        - Who are our primary clients/customers?
        - What unique value do we provide to our clients?
        - How do we make a difference in their lives or businesses?
        - What are our fundamental values and principles that guide our work?
        - If we disappeared tomorrow, what would our clients and employees miss most?
    - **Activity:** Individual brainstorming on sticky notes based on guiding questions, group discussion, and clustering of similar ideas. Begin drafting initial mission statement phrases.

- **3. Understanding "Vision" (60 mins):**
    - **Definition:** "Where are we going? What does success look like in the future (e.g., 5-10 years from now)? What kind of impact do we aspire to have?"
    - **Guiding Questions for Discussion:**
        - What is our ultimate aspiration as an agent company?
        - What does our ideal future state look like? (e.g., market position, client reputation, company size, employee culture)
        - How will we have transformed the industry or our clients' experiences?
        - What legacy do we want to leave?
        - If we achieve all our goals, what will be the ultimate impact?
    - **Activity:** Individual brainstorming, group discussion, and clustering. Begin drafting initial vision statement phrases.

- **4. Drafting & Review (60 mins):**
    - **Activity:** Collaborate to combine the best ideas into concise, impactful draft mission and vision statements.
    - **Review Criteria:** Is it clear? Is it inspiring? Is it memorable? Does it differentiate us? Is it aspirational yet achievable?
    - **Action:** Assign a small task force (2-3 participants) to refine these drafts before Workshop 2.

## Workshop 2: Long-Term Strategic Objectives Development (Half-Day)

**Materials Needed:** Whiteboard or flip charts, markers, sticky notes, copies of refined draft Mission and Vision statements.

**Agenda:**

- **1. Review & Feedback on Mission/Vision (30 mins):**
    - Present the refined draft mission and vision statements from the task force.
    - Open floor for final feedback and minor adjustments. Aim for consensus.

- **2. Understanding "Strategic Objectives" (60 mins):**
    - **Definition:** "What are the 3-5 most critical, measurable, long-term goals that will help us achieve our vision and fulfill our mission?" These should be high-level, overarching goals, not day-to-day tasks.
    - **Guiding Questions for Discussion:**
        - Given our mission and vision, what are the most critical areas we must excel in over the next 3-5 years?
        - What major accomplishments would signify significant progress towards our vision?
        - How will we measure our success in achieving our vision?
        - Consider areas like market growth, client satisfaction, operational excellence, financial performance, talent development, etc.
    - **Activity:** Individual brainstorming of potential strategic objectives, followed by group discussion and consolidation into a list.

- **3. Refining Strategic Objectives (60 mins):**
    - **Activity:** Apply the SMART criteria (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) to each proposed objective.
    - **Example:** Instead of "Increase sales," refine to "Achieve a 25% increase in market share in the B2B agent services sector within the next three years."
    - Prioritize the top 3-5 objectives that are most impactful and aligned with the mission/vision.

- **4. Next Steps & Communication (30 mins):**
    - **Action:** Assign ownership for each strategic objective.
    - Discuss initial ideas for communicating the finalized mission, vision, and objectives to the broader team.
    - Emphasize that these objectives will form the basis for departmental and individual goals.
